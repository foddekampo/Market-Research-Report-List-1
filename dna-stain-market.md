<p><p>The DNA stain market comprises several key types, each with unique applications. Ethidium Bromide is widely used for visualizing DNA in gel electrophoresis due to its high sensitivity. SYBR Stain offers enhanced fluorescence and is utilized in quantitative PCR. Propidium Iodide is effective for staining dead cells, providing insight into viability. Hoechst Dye binds to adenine-thymine-rich regions, allowing for cell cycle studies. Other stains may include fluorescent dyes used in specialized applications, catering to diverse research needs in molecular biology.</p></p>
