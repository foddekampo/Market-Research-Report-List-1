<p><p>Activated carbon for water treatment is categorized into two main types: powder activated carbon (PAC) and granular activated carbon (GAC). PAC is fine, with a high surface area, mainly used for small-scale applications, effectively adsorbing contaminants in water. GAC, larger in particle size, is ideal for larger systems like municipal water treatment plants, offering prolonged contact time and reusability in filtration systems. Both types play crucial roles in removing impurities, organic compounds, and pollutants, ensuring cleaner water.</p></p>
