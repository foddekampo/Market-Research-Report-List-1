<p><p>Fluorocarbon surfactants are classified into four main types based on their charge properties. Amphoteric surfactants can exhibit both positive and negative charges, providing versatility in various applications. Anionic surfactants bear a negative charge, offering strong cleaning and foaming capabilities, commonly used in detergents. Cationic surfactants possess a positive charge, known for antimicrobial properties and often used in fabric softeners. Nonionic surfactants, lacking a charge, are valued for their stability and mildness, making them ideal for personal care and industrial formulations.</p></p>
