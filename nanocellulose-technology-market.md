<p><p>Nanocellulose technology encompasses three primary market types: cellulose nanofibrils (CNF), cellulose nanocrystals (CNC), and bacterial nanocellulose (BNC). CNF consists of long, flexible fibers with high surface area, excellent mechanical properties, and is often used in composite materials. CNC comprises rigid, crystalline structures, offering strength and reinforcement for various applications. BNC, produced through bacterial fermentation, is characterized by its high purity and water retention, making it valuable in biomedical and food industries. Together, they represent innovative biobased materials for diverse applications.</p></p>
